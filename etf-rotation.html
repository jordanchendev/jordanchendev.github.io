ETF Rotation: what 189 strategies and three validation layers actually reveal ETF 輪動:189 個策略與三層驗證,到底告訴我們什麼

Leveraged ETFs promise spectacular returns. The interesting question isn't whether they're high-return — it's whether any rule for rotating between a core ETF and its leveraged sibling is worth actually executing, once you account for drawdown, trading frequency, and luck. 槓桿 ETF 帳面報酬很誘人。真正有趣的問題不是「報酬高不高」,而是:在核心 ETF 與它的槓桿版本之間做輪動的規則,扣掉回撤、交易頻率與運氣之後,到底值不值得真的執行?

1. The question 一、問題

I backtested six core/leveraged ETF pairs — three US (QQQ/TQQQ, VOO/SSO, VOO/UPRO, SPY/UPRO) and two Taiwan (0050/00631L, 006204/00675L) — across every strategy I could define: buy-the-dip leverage, reverse de-risking, a 2x-plus-cash band, moving-average rules, and switch-to-cash variants. That's 189 strategies in total. Then I ran three independent validation layers to check whether each result was real or just a lucky fit to one slice of history.

我回測了六組「核心 / 槓桿」ETF 對——三組美股(QQQ/TQQQ、VOO/SSO、VOO/UPRO、SPY/UPRO)與兩組台股(0050/00631L、006204/00675L)—— 並套上所有我能定義的策略:買跌加槓桿、反向降槓桿、正2 + 現金權重帶、均線規則、切現金等,共 189 個策略。 接著用三層獨立驗證,檢查每個結果是真的穩健,還是只是剛好 fit 到某一段歷史。

The thesis up front: the goal was never to find the highest backtest number. It was to judge whether a rotation rule holds up across return, drawdown, trading cost, and different market conditions. The most impressive backtest is usually the least trustworthy. 先講結論:目標從來不是找出「歷史最高報酬」,而是判斷一條輪動規則在報酬、回撤、交易成本與不同市況下是否站得住腳。 最亮眼的回測,通常最不可信。

2. A reality check on the benchmarks 二、先看兩條基準

Every comparison is anchored on two lines: holding 100% of the core ETF, and holding 100% of the leveraged ETF. The leveraged buy-and-hold numbers are where most people stop reading — and where most people would have been shaken out.

每個比較都錨定在兩條線上:全程持有 100% 核心 ETF,以及全程持有 100% 槓桿 ETF。槓桿純持有的數字,是多數人停止閱讀的地方—— 也是多數人實際上會被洗出場的地方。

TQQQ compounded at 43.8% a year — and drew down −81.7%. A −80% drawdown means watching $100k become $18k and holding on. Almost nobody does.

TQQQ 年化 43.8%——但最大回撤 −81.7%。 −80% 回撤的意思是:看著 100 萬變成 18 萬還抱得住。幾乎沒有人做得到。

3. Insight #1 — a little well-timed leverage beats buy-and-hold 三、洞察 1:少量、擇時的槓桿,勝過無腦持有

The best composite strategy on QQQ/TQQQ spends 95% of the time in plain QQQ and only 4.5% in TQQQ. With just 2 switches in 16 years it lifts CAGR from 19.8% to 23.9% while keeping drawdown near the core (−40% vs −35%). The two switches were disciplined and rare: into leverage after QQQ fell 35% from its high (Oct 2022), back to core after it rebounded 45% (Jul 2023). Buy the crash, not the top.

QQQ/TQQQ 的「綜合分數最佳」策略,有 95% 的時間只持有 QQQ,只有 4.5% 進 TQQQ。 16 年只切換 2 次,就把年化從 19.8% 拉到 23.9%,而回撤仍貼近核心(−40% vs −35%)。 那兩次切換又少又有紀律:QQQ 從高點跌 35% 時進槓桿(2022 年 10 月),反彈 45% 後回核心(2023 年 7 月)。買崩盤,不買高點。

4. Insight #2 — drawdown is the binding constraint 四、洞察 2:回撤,才是真正的天花板

Once you rank strategies by what you can actually survive, the picture changes. The "highest return within a −40% drawdown" family is the sweet spot — and Taiwan's pairs shine, because Taiwan's leveraged ETFs draw down far less (−55%) than US 3x products (−77% to −82%). On 006204/00675L, reverse de-risking earns 41.1% CAGR at −43.8% drawdown, versus 21.2% for the core — roughly double the return for a controlled risk budget.

一旦改用「你實際抱得住什麼」來排序,畫面就變了。「回撤 −40% 內最高報酬」這一族是甜蜜點——而台股組特別亮眼, 因為台股槓桿 ETF 的回撤(−55%)遠比美股 3x 產品(−77% ~ −82%)溫和。006204/00675L 的反向降槓桿達到 41.1% 年化、回撤僅 −43.8%,而核心只有 21.2%——在受控的風險預算下,報酬約為兩倍。

5. Insight #3 — one moving-average line goes a long way 五、洞察 3:一條均線,就走得很遠

The dead-simple rule "hold leverage only while the core is above its SMA200" captures a surprising amount: 39.5% CAGR on QQQ/TQQQ versus 19.8% for the core. The catch is cost: it trades 87 times and rides a −59% drawdown. Simplicity and robustness, paid for in turnover.

「核心站上 SMA200 才持有槓桿」這條極簡規則,抓到的東西意外地多:QQQ/TQQQ 年化 39.5%, 核心才 19.8%。代價是成本:它切換了 87 次,並承受 −59% 回撤。簡單又穩健,但用周轉率換來。

6. The overfitting trap 六、過度配適的陷阱

The "highest historical return" strategy on QQQ/TQQQ shows a jaw-dropping 487× final multiple (46% CAGR). It gets there with 21 switches perfectly placed on this one historical path. That's not a strategy you can trust — it's a curve fit. This is exactly why a single backtest, however gorgeous, can't be the conclusion. It has to survive validation.

QQQ/TQQQ 的「歷史報酬最高」策略,帳面終值高達 487 倍(46% 年化)。它靠的是 21 次剛好踩在這條歷史路徑上的切換。 這不是能信任的策略,而是一條曲線配適。這正是為何單一回測再漂亮也不能當結論——它必須通過驗證。

7. Three layers of validation 七、三層驗證

Each layer attacks the same question from a different angle: is this strategy good, or was it just lucky?

每一層都從不同角度攻擊同一個問題:這個策略是真的好,還是只是運氣好?

Layer 1 — Rolling entry 第一層 — 滾動進場

Shift the entry month forward across 180+ windows. A revealing result: the "best composite" strategy, so impressive on full history, beats the core in only 32% of 5-year rolling windows — it's entry-timing dependent. By contrast, the "≤−40% drawdown" strategy beats core in 100% of 5-year windows with a median 2.83× and a P05 of 2.20×. That's robustness.

把進場月份往後平移,跑 180+ 個窗口。一個很有啟發的結果:在完整歷史上很亮眼的「綜合分數最佳」,在 5 年滾動窗只有 32% 勝過核心——它很吃進場時機。相對地,「回撤 −40% 內」策略在 5 年窗口 100% 勝過核心,中位 2.83 倍、P05 也有 2.20 倍。這才是穩健。

Layer 2 — Market regimes 第二層 — 市況分組

Group entry points by what the market was doing — bull, bear, sideways — to see whether a strategy is a one-trick pony that only works entering from a crash. (Some regime samples are small, so this is a supporting check, not a verdict on its own.)

依進場前的行情把進場點分成牛市、熊市、盤整,看一個策略是不是只在「從崩盤進場」時有效的偏食策略。 (部分市況樣本偏少,所以這層是輔助判斷,不單獨當結論。)

Layer 3 — Monte Carlo 第三層 — 蒙地卡羅

Resample historical monthly returns into 5,000 simulated 10-year paths. This is where the leverage fantasy meets reality: pure TQQQ has a dazzling median of 36×, but a P05 of just 2.07× (barely above core), a 2.4% chance of a 10-year loss, and a median drawdown of −63%. The "≤−40% drawdown" strategy gives a median 9.0× with a P05 of 2.5×, a 0.2% loss chance, and a −30% median drawdown — far more livable.

把歷史月報酬重抽樣,組成 5,000 條 10 年模擬路徑。這裡正是槓桿幻想撞上現實的地方:純 TQQQ 中位數高達 36 倍, 但 P05 只有 2.07 倍(僅略高於核心)、10 年虧損機率 2.4%、中位回撤 −63%。「回撤 −40% 內」策略中位 9.0 倍、P05 2.5 倍、 虧損機率 0.2%、中位回撤 −30%——體驗可承受得多。

Tall P95 with a low P05 means big upside bought with a painful path — read the spread, not just the median. P95 很高但 P05 很低,代表上方空間大、但過程很痛——要看分佈區間,不要只看中位數。

8. What I take away 八、我的結論

Three things hold across all six pairs. First, drawdown — not return — decides what you can actually hold, so rank by survivable risk first. Second, a small, rules-based dose of leverage beats both buy-and-hold core and full leverage on a risk-adjusted basis. Third, and most important, the prettiest backtest is the least trustworthy; only strategies that survive rolling entry, regime grouping, and Monte Carlo earn any confidence.

三件事在六組標的上都成立。第一,決定你抱不抱得住的是回撤,不是報酬,所以要先用可承受的風險來排序。 第二,少量、有規則的槓桿,在風險調整後勝過無腦持有核心、也勝過滿倉槓桿。第三,也最重要: 最漂亮的回測最不可信;只有同時通過滾動進場、市況分組與蒙地卡羅的策略,才值得一點信心。
